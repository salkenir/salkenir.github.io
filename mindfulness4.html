How to Improve Self-Esteem

If you are experiencing low self-esteem, such as, negative self talk, practicing mindfulness and meditation is a great start to changing the way your mind works. Changing your relationship with your thoughts promotes self-confidence and self-worth.

"Self-esteem is how we value ourselves — thoughts and stories that provide the running commentary about the kind of person we think we are. If that inner-voice is always saying things like “I’m no good” or “I do everything wrong” or “No one likes me,” then a poor self-regard might well be impacting other aspects of your life and relationships."
- Headspace.com

  • Practice self-compassion: treating yourself with love and kindess can allow us to let go of hostility or judgement. Learn to forgive yourself, you are human.
  • Affirm your real-worth: try to see yourself from someone else's POV. Write down a list of compliments other's have given you. Think of your accomplishments, whether that be in your job, school, or personal life. What do those accomplishments say about you?
  • Get up and get moving: Staying stagnant and allowing yourself to sit with your thoughts for too long can result in low self-esteem and a draining cycle. Get outside and learn something new, hold yourself to a new goal and do what it takes to complete it. This will result in building your self-worth as well as trust in yourself.
