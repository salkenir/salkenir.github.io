Sleep Hacks

Do you have trouble falling asleep and staying asleep? Sleep is a vital part of our health and is a period where our brain builds itself and grows, use these hacks to take control of your sleep.

"Self-esteem is how we value ourselves — thoughts and stories that provide the running commentary about the kind of person we think we are. If that inner-voice is always saying things like “I’m no good” or “I do everything wrong” or “No one likes me,” then a poor self-regard might well be impacting other aspects of your life and relationships."
- Headspace.com

  • Be consistent: Try setting a consistent night-time routine. Set a time you would like to be in bed by every night, and try to stick to it.
  • Limit your caffeine: Caffeine is a huge instigator of anxiety, let alone it can play a huge role in how you fall asleep. If you cannot part with caffeine entirely, try cutting off your intake at least mid-day.
  • Get up and get moving: I suffered from insomnia for a long time, and the one thing that made me notice the most difference in my sleep quality was exercising. On the days I would work out, I noticed I was falling asleep way easier at night. This goes for once I started working and going to school, by the end of the day I am ready for bed, no questions asked!
