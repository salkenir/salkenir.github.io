Sleeping with your phone

Do you find yourself scrolling your social media feeds before bed? I personally am guilty of this, and I know plenty of others who are as well. Our phones are such a huge part of our day, especially for people in the tech world. It is extremely important to seperate phone time and sleep time, doing so will actually help you fall asleep faster.

Light

Your phone, tablet, and computer all emit blue light.


"Blue light is a color in the visible light spectrum that can be seen by human eyes. Blue light is a short wavelength, which means it produces higher amounts of energy."
- bluetechlenses.com

Blue light has been proven to disrupt the sleep/wake cycle (circadian rhythm). Excessive blue light exposure can lead to sleep and mood disorders, leading to depression.

Increased awareness at night

Say you read an email before bed, one that was worded strongly, or just didn't sit right with you. Now you are up with your mind racing, thinking about the email you just read and before you know it hours have passed.


Consider putting your phone into "sleep mode" or "do not disturb", you can even set a schedule for when this feature cuts on. The nice thign about both of these settings is you can choose who you want to still be able to recieve calls from, incase of an emergency.

If you are looking to improve your sleep quality, consider ditching the phone at least an hour or two before you get into bed,and watch your sleep transform!
