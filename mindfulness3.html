Mindful Cooking

Something as mundane as cooking can be turned into an opportunity to practice mindfulness. Ask yourself, where is your mind at as you add each ingredient, slicing, pouring, and stirring. A lot of time our minds are focused on every other thing going on in our lives, rather than being present.

How does mindful cooking work?

When practicing mindfulness, the goal is to be present. To do that you must take in all the details around you in that current moment. This applies for cooking as well. Acknowledge every smell, color, and texture you experience. When you are following a recipe, stay present in each step of the process. As we do this, our awareness grows and the process becomes meditative and calming.

“Whether it’s the warmth of the sunshine on our back, the sound of the food sizzling on the grill, or the sweet smell of spices wafting in the breeze, cooking outdoors can be a magical affair. With such a rich environment for our senses, grilling out is the perfect activity to remind ourselves of what it means to be mindful — relaxed in the body, at ease in the mind and just that little bit more connected with the world around us.”
- Andy Piddicombe, Co-founder of Headspace
