How to stop overthinking

Your thoughts can be your own worst enemy. I personally find myself overthinking almost every day, but simply learning how to identify these thoughts can make a huge difference.

What triggers overthinking?

The two basic things that underly overthinking are stress and anxiety. The next big thing that comes into play is self-esteem. People with low self-esteem are more liekly to be less confident in themselves and their decisions, resulting in overthinking.

Destructive thought patterns

Overthinking often involves dwelling on the past and incessant worrying.

  • I shouldn’t have said those things at the work meeting yesterday. Everyone must think I’m an idiot.
  • I should have stayed at my last school. I would be happier than I am now.
  • Why did I post that? Everyone probably thinks I am annoying.

Persistant worrying involves negative predictions about the future. Thoughts may look like this:

  • I am going to look so stupid at the presentation tomorrow, what if i forget everything I practiced?
  • Everyone else will get chosen before me.
  • I know I'll never be able to make it out to New York. I will never have enough money.

    • How to break the cycle

    You aren't stuck dealing with these repetitive thoughts, and there are ways to train your brain to think differently.

    • Notice when you're stuck in your own head: Overthinking can become such a habit that you don’t even recognize when you’re doing it. Start paying attention to the way you think so you can become aware of the problem.
    • Challenge your thoughts: It’s easy to get carried away with negative thoughts. So, before you conclude that calling in sick is going to get you fired, or that forgetting one deadline will cause you to become homeless, acknowledge that your thoughts may be exaggeratedly negative. Simply asking myself if those thoughts are valid helps me to realize I am overthinking.
    • Change the channel: Change the channel in your brain by going to do another activity. Exercise, engage in conversation on a completely different subject, or work on a project that distracts you. Doing something different will put an end to the barrage of negative thoughts.
